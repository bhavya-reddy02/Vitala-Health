# Building healthy habits with Vitala

Lasting health change comes from small, repeatable habits rather than short bursts of willpower. This guide covers how to build habits and how Vitala's features support them.

## How habits form
Habits get easier when they are small, specific, and attached to something you already do. Instead of a vague goal like "exercise more", a tiny, clear action such as "a five-minute walk after lunch" is far easier to repeat. Linking a new habit to an existing routine — this is sometimes called habit stacking — gives it a reliable trigger. Making the action easy to start lowers the barrier on low-energy days.

## Consistency over intensity
Doing a little, often, beats doing a lot occasionally. Missing a day is normal and not a failure; the important thing is to return the next day rather than abandon the habit. Self-compassion supports consistency far better than harsh self-criticism.

## Tracking and rewards
Seeing progress is motivating. Tracking a habit — even just marking it done — creates a small sense of achievement, and short feedback loops help the habit stick. Celebrating small wins keeps momentum going.

## How Vitala helps
Vitala turns healthy habits into a game so returning each day feels rewarding:
- Daily quests are small, achievable actions drawn from your chosen focus areas, such as drinking water, moving, or winding down before bed.
- Completing quests earns XP, and XP builds your level, giving a visible sense of progress.
- Streaks count the days in a row you stay active, encouraging consistency; keeping a streak alive only takes one completed quest a day.
- Badges mark milestones, such as your first quest, a seven-day streak, or a flawless day where every quest is done.
- The leaderboard offers a bit of friendly motivation by ranking players by total XP.

## Using it well
Choose two or three focus areas that matter to you so quests feel relevant. Aim to complete at least one quest a day to protect your streak, and treat perfect days as a bonus rather than a pressure. The goal is steady, enjoyable progress that fits your life.

This is general wellbeing information, not medical advice.
